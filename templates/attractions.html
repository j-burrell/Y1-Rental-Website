Shopping

Norwich is fairly compact, compared to other city's in the UK and therefore makes a good choice for a weekend shopping spree as all the main areas can be accessed easily on foot. You will find some lovely streets and alleys linking the main areas, containing all kinds of shops, from chain stores to individual boutiques.

Sightseeing

One of the best ways to feel at home in a destination is to live like a local and nothing sets you up better to live like a local than some of Norwich’s best attractions Get under the skin of some of Norwich’s most famous and iconic buildings. Norwich has two cathedrals; the first is the beautiful 900 year old Romanesque Norwich Cathedral, bursting at the seams with history. Norwich Castle Museum & Art Gallery sits proudly on its mound high above the city, just a few years older than Norwich Cathedral. This museum houses all matter of artifacts from Saxon gold hoards and jewellery, to a vast collection of tea pots and stunning natural history displays. There are frequent special exhibitions, tours, talks and activities plus battlements and a dungeon. Norwich has many more sights to offer to explore either alone or with friends and family

Restaurants

The historic architecture, cobbled lanes and leafy riverside are just perfect to stroll through, which means you'll fast work up an appetite. Norwich has a thriving food scene and with so many restaurants and cafes to choose from, it can be a little overwhelming. Luckily, Norwich has plenty of places in which to grab a satisfying bite. From pizza to sushi to tacos, it’s all here.
